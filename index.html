The Project

Babalift develops handmade multi-seat bicycles crafted from recycled materials and open-source ideas. What began as a journey to give rides and interview anyone on peace and non-violence in Africa evolved into a broader vision of inclusive mobility: bicycles designed not only for transport, but for participation, dignity, connection and joy. Every prototype is built through experimentation, collaboration and solidarity.

About

Mission

To create inclusive, open-source mobility solutions that bring people together through shared journeys, freedom of movement and human connection.

Vision

A world where mobility is accessible, joyful and community-driven — built by people, for people.

Values

  • Solidarity over profit
  • Human connection through movement
  • Open-source creativity
  • Reuse and sustainability
  • Inclusion and accessibility
  • Handmade innovation
  • Shared experiences across cultures

Goals

  • Build affordable multi-seat bicycles using recycled materials
  • Support mobility for children and people with disabilities
  • Share open-source designs worldwide
  • Train local communities in fabrication and repair
  • Connect people through shared rides and collaborative journeys
  • Expand the project across Africa, Europe and Asia

Cooperation

In the past 16 years we have cooperated with many realities.

Partnered with organizations and companies. Sponsoring NGOs and associations including ELOI, Red Cross, Co.P.E., Mondo Sostenibile, BlockOut, Couchsurfing, ESN, and many more.
