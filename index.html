The Project

BaBaLift is far more than an exercise in mechanical design, fitness, or environmental sustainability. It is a radical initiative in open-source mobility engineered to dismantle isolation and rebuild deep human connection across communities worldwide. We build multi-seat, adaptive tandem frames entirely FROM RECYCLED PARTS AND REUSED MATERIALS, proving that resourcefulness can fuel profound social transformation.

Our frameworks are meticulously designed to lift absolutely anyone—reclaiming independent movement for individuals with physical disabilities, providing guided spatial freedom for the blind, and restoring community presence for the elderly. The balanced geometry makes the vehicle uniquely approachable: its specialized layout ensures that even young children can comfortably pilot the chassis or enjoy the journey from the front seat, transforming mobility into intuitive, shared play.

A Proven Legacy of Journeying: Our very first hand-built prototype traveled over 15,000 kilometers and provided more than 10,000 community rides across challenging routes, proving the real-world durability of open-source engineering while actively promoting sport for peace. Today, this mission continues across active prototypes operating in Africa, Europe, and our newly fabricated initial chassis in India, which is already completing kilometers and delivering vital shared rides daily.

About Us

Mission

To create inclusive, open-source mobility solutions that bring people together through shared journeys, freedom of movement and human connection.

Vision

A world where mobility is accessible, joyful and community-driven — built by people, for people.

Values

  • Solidarity over profit: Placing human value and community integration above commercial gain.
  • Human connection through movement: Using physical journeys to foster mutual trust and break down cross-cultural social barriers.
  • Open-source creativity: Sharing technical blueprints freely so communities worldwide can replicate and build independently.
  • Reuse and sustainability: Hand-crafting long-lasting transport solutions directly from discarded parts and structural steel elements.
  • Inclusion and accessibility: Designing vehicles that adapt directly to senior citizens, children, and blind or disabled passengers.

Goals

  • Build affordable multi-seat bicycles using recycled materials
  • Support mobility for children and people with disabilities
  • Share open-source designs worldwide
  • Train local communities in fabrication and repair
  • Connect people through shared rides and collaborative journeys
  • Expand the project across Africa, Europe and Asia
